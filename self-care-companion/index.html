🫣 Avoidance ▾

We avoid tasks not because we're lazy — but because we're afraid. Afraid that trying and not succeeding says something about who we are. Afraid the outcome might confirm that quiet inner voice: "maybe I'm not enough."

We tell ourselves: "Oh, if I really wanted to, I would have done it by now."

But the truth is gentler than that. We're scared. Scared to find out the task might need more from us. And that is completely okay. More work just means more room to grow.

🦁 Courage — the leap of faith ▾

The antidote to avoidance isn't motivation. It's courage — small, quiet, and real.

"Life expands or shrinks in proportion to our courage."

You don't need to feel ready. You don't need to be perfect. You just need to begin. The leap is the point — not the landing. Start anyway. Every time you do, life gets a little bigger.

📊 Prioritise & execute ▾

Not all tasks deserve the same energy at the same time. Before diving in, ask two questions: Is this important? Is this urgent? The answers will tell you exactly where to start.
